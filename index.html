Game: Plants vs Zombie

Python

Overview

This is the term project for CMU 15-112: Fundamentals of Programming. In this TP, my goal was to rebuild the popular tower-defense game, Plants vs Zombies using Python and the CMU 112 graphics package. The game was originally developed by PopCap Games and published by Electronic Arts. I will make a simplified version of that, which will only include a few types of plants, zombies, and two levels (the day level and the night level). The player's goal is to successfully defeat waves of zombies using the plants that are provided for each level.


Level descriptions:

Normal Level:

The player will be provided five types of plants (sunflower, peashooter, walnut and potato mine, kernel pult). The player needs to defend 3 waves of zombies.


Hard Level:

The player will be provided four types of plants (sunflower, peashooter, walnut, and kernel pult). The number of waves increase to 4 waves.

Game: Tetris

Python

Overview

This is the one of the game projects for CMU 15-112: Fundamentals of Programming. The goal is to make a simple Tetris game.


The tetris design includes two main elements: a board and a falling piece. The goal of Tetris is to score as many points as possible by clearing horizontal lines of Blocks.


Game Instructions:

  • rotate the falling piece by pressing the left, top, right and bottom keys
  • "hard-drop" key, which by hitting the space bar immediately drops a piece as far as it can go and places it on the board
  • player will recieve one point for clearing the bottom of the falling pieces
  • start a new game at any time by hitting 'r' for 'restart'

Overview

Motivation

It all began with the idea of organizing my portfolio since my major was architecture. But when going through all those popular websites for portfolio making, I wasn't satisfied with the template they provided. Also, I didn't like to have ".wix.com" as such a name on my website URL (overall, having to pay monthly subscriptions, etc made me I felt that my freedom in design was limited). That's why this website was born!

Besides that, I also want to have a place to record all the cool projects I've worked on. I used to have all my projects stored in a folder on my USB, but how long till that USB is left in some corners of my house and then is gone forever. After 10 or 20 years, it would be a shame if I don't have a collection of the projects and memories of the people I have worked with. I hope to make this website my journey of growth.

First Iteration

The first iteration was developed in the Coursera web development course offered by the University of Michigan. I developed a very simple website for the course assignment.

It was also my first time learning about HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. As you can see, it doesn't look like a website but is more of a pdf resume.

Current Iteration

Intending to build a more "professional" looking website and want to learn more in-depth about the web development language, I took the STUCO course (Building your Website) offered at my university. Then I started to redesign the site from scratch.

Redesign Maybank Website

HTML/CSS/JavaScript

Overview

I still remember the day when I opened the Maybank website just want to open my bank account, yet I was shocked by how careless and disastrous their website design was. Maybank is one of Malaysia's biggest banking service providers, but its main web page is destroying that title. Their webpage has hunted me for a long time, and when I got to learn HTML, CSS, and JS, I felt that I finally have the chance to do something about their website (even though it won't change the difference between their website, but at least I think I could have a better design than the current one).


Hence, I've decided to take on this project as a personal interested to redesign the website.


Table of Contents

  • Background
  • Problems
  • My Proposal
  • Conclusion

Background

Maybank is a Malaysian universal bank, with the key markets in Singapore, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It is Malaysia's largest bank by market capitalization and total assets and one of the largest banks in Southeast Asia.


Despite this great influence in Malaysia, Maybank's website is constantly facing negative reviews from consumers.


So, what is the problem?


Problems


You can expereince it first-hand here.
**UPDATE: The official website has been updated while this project was done in March 2022. Therefore the description and the image below might not look like what the current official MayBank website is.



This is the front page of Maybank website. As you can see, the site contains so many components and buttons. I got a headache by reading all of these features. It really left me with an impression of a school project done at the last minute.


Upon reviewing the website, I concluded three major problems with the design:



The horizontal scrolling:


It's hard to navigate and especially when they have an unusual horizontal scrolling design, which already confused so many users from the start.



The dropdown navigation:


Having a gigantic universal blackhole of the dropdown menu is also a minus to the design. There are so many information given to me and also with the color of the background, it really becomes unpleasant to read.



Lack of target consumer group:


When I entered the main page intending to open a bank account, I was genuinely lost in this overwhelming content and not sure where to click. All the titles and boxes are designed in a similar format and it is hard to find the main target from this site. Just try to imagine, an investor trying to invest in this bank or a student wanting to get an internship from the website. They might already move on to the next company when seeing this site.


My Proposal

New Website Design

The intention for this site design is to keep it simple and intuitive since Maybank targets user groups with all sorts of demographic. Therefore, I first changed the scrolling style to a vertical one. Then I spilt the main page into three sections: the first one is the landing page showing all the categories the users want to search for from the bank. Moving on to the second section, we will see the recent news and a convenient service section that provides quick navigation for users. Then the last section is the “call to action” section, which includes donation ads, and new events from the bank that draws users' attention.

Conclusion

Of course, this is not the perfect design solution for Maybank, and I know there needs to be real user testing and feedback to achieve the result. Therefore, I will continue to learn new skills to improve the web experience and also updating the design styles.
